## Why

The platform has no front door for researchers, university evaluators, or policy analysts. The "Who It's For" page on findabed.org addresses shelter coordinators, CoC administrators, city officials, developers, and funders — but not the academic and research community that could validate the platform's impact, publish findings, and provide the evidence base that funders and government adopters require.

FABT captures research-grade operational data that doesn't exist in traditional HMIS: real-time unmet demand signals (zero-result bed searches by population type), utilization curves at sub-daily granularity, reservation lifecycle data (hold-to-confirmation conversion rates), and DV referral response times — all with zero client PII by design. This data could answer questions HUD and NAEH have been asking: How much shelter demand goes unmeasured? What's the actual gap between available beds and people seeking them?

DVBeds (The National DV Hotline, 19 states, 50,000+ annual logins) is the closest comparable system but focuses on DV-only and doesn't publish research data. The LA Controller audit found 25% of LAHSA shelter beds sat empty due to "severe data quality issues" — exactly the problem FABT's real-time model solves. Researchers evaluating shelter coordination systems have nowhere to go right now.

The 2026 NAEH conference submission window closed March 17, 2026. A FOR-RESEARCHERS page + pilot data could target the 2027 cycle. Dr. Yemi Okafor (research methodologist persona) and Dr. Kenji Watanabe (HUD policy persona) both identified this gap independently.
