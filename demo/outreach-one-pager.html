Finding A Bed Tonight

No more midnight phone calls.

A family in crisis needs an emergency shelter bed. Right now, outreach workers make phone calls — to shelters that may be full, closed, or unable to serve that family. We built a free platform that shows real-time availability so workers can search, hold a bed, and get the family there safely.

For Outreach Workers

  • Search available beds by population type on your phone — see what's open right now
  • Hold a bed in three taps while you transport the client (default 90 minutes, configurable)
  • Works offline — your search results stay available if you lose signal

For Shelter Coordinators

  • Update bed count in about 30 seconds — three taps from the coordinator dashboard
  • See active holds so you know who's coming and when
  • Partial participation — you can start with bed counts only, no reservations required

For DV Shelters

  • Zero client information stored — no names, no addresses, no identifying data, ever
  • Shelter address never displayed — shared verbally during a phone call only
  • All referral data permanently deleted within 24 hours

For Reentry Navigators (v0.55+)

  • Find transitional shelters that accept clients with felony histories — search by criminal-record policy and program-specific eligibility
  • Place a hold for a future release using the same 3-tap flow; optional client name and date of birth are encrypted at rest and erased no later than 25 hours after the hold ends (CoC opt-in via reentryMode tenant flag)
  • See per-shelter eligibility criteria so you can match a client to the right placement before the call

See the reentry walkthrough for the operator-facing flow.

What It Costs

The software is free and open-source (Apache 2.0 license). Hosting costs depend on your community's size: $15–30/month for a small community (PostgreSQL only), $30–75 for mid-size, $100+ for metro areas. Your CoC or city typically covers hosting.

Where We Are

The platform is built, tested, and live as a demonstration deployment at findabed.org with fictional seed data — but not yet adopted by any CoC community for production use. We're looking for a first CoC partner to pilot with. What's still in progress: email-based "forgot password" recovery (admins reset credentials manually today) and a third-party accessibility audit. We name our gaps because trust matters more than polish.
