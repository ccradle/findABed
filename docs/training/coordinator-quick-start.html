Finding A Bed Tonight

Quick-Start for Shelter Coordinators
Your job: keep your bed count up to date.

How to Update Your Bed Count

1
Open the app in your browser
Your CoC administrator will give you the web address.
2
Log in with three things:
Tenant: your organization's code (ask your CoC admin if you don't know it)
Email: your work email
Password: the password your CoC admin gave you
3
Find your shelter on the dashboard and tap to expand it
4
Update the numbers — use the + and − buttons for Total Beds and Occupied
The "On Hold" number is automatic — outreach workers set it when they reserve a bed. Don't change it.
5
Tap Save
✓ You'll see a green confirmation when it saves. If you don't see it, try again — duplicate saves are harmless.
How often? At shift change and any time a large group arrives or leaves. Twice a day is a good rhythm.

Need to Edit Your Shelter's Details?

Phone number changed? Hours shifted? Expand your shelter card and tap Edit Details. You can update phone, hours, and operational info. Name, address, and DV status are managed by your CoC admin.

What the Freshness Badges Mean

Outreach workers see these badges next to your shelter. They make decisions based on them.

FRESH Updated in the last hour. Outreach workers trust this data.
AGING 1–8 hours old. Data may be outdated.
STALE 8+ hours old. Outreach workers will call before driving.

If Something Goes Wrong

Common issues and what to do

Can't log in?

Check your tenant code — it's case-sensitive. If you don't know it, ask your CoC administrator. Also check your email spelling. If your password isn't working, your CoC admin can reset it from the Users tab.

Not sure if it saved?

Look for the green ✓ confirmation after tapping Save. If you don't see it, tap Save again — saving the same numbers twice is harmless. The bed count on the dashboard should update immediately.

App says you're offline?

Check your internet connection. The app saves your work locally — when you reconnect, it sends the update automatically. You can also try using your phone's mobile data if the desktop WiFi is down.

Numbers don't look right?

Available beds = Total − Occupied − On Hold. The "On Hold" number comes from outreach worker reservations and updates automatically. If Available shows a negative number, your Occupied count may be too high — reduce it and save.

Need to close your shelter temporarily?

Contact your CoC administrator — they can mark it inactive. You don't need to delete anything.
